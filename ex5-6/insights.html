Energy Insights

Discover the latest trends and insights in TV energy consumption. We analyze data from the Australian government's energy rating website to bring you valuable information on how TVs are evolving in terms of energy efficiency.

Trends in Energy Efficiency

Over the past few years, there has been a significant improvement in the energy efficiency of televisions. Manufacturers are adopting new technologies that reduce power consumption while maintaining high performance and picture quality.

Most Frequent Screen Technology in Australia

See which screen technology consumes the most energy:

This donut chart compares annual energy consumption among LCD, LED, and OLED televisions. OLED has the highest consumption, followed by LED, then LCD. Exact percentages are shown as labels on the chart and are also available in the chart tooltip.

Screen Size vs Energy Consumption

Want to know the relationships between Screen Size and Energy Consumption? Let us tell you.

This scatter plot shows the relationship between TV screen size (in inches) and energy consumption (in kWh/year). Each point represents a TV model, with the x-axis representing screen size and the y-axis representing energy consumption. The plot reveals a positive correlation, indicating that larger TVs tend to consume more energy. Exact values can be seen in the tooltip when hovering over each point.

Energy consumption by screen size

This bar chart displays energy consumption for different screen sizes. Each bar represents a screen size category, and the height of the bar indicates the average energy consumption for that category. The chart helps visualize how energy consumption varies across different screen sizes.

Star rating for different screen sizes

This line chart displays star ratings for different screen sizes. Each line represents a different screen size category, and the points on the line show the average star rating for that category. The chart helps visualize how star ratings vary across different screen sizes.
